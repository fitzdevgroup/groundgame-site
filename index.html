The Problem

Your political program is more sophisticated than your tools.

Unions have been running serious political operations for decades — but the software catching up to that work has mostly been built for campaigns or corporations, not for the way unions actually operate.

Where does your legislator relationship data actually live?

Stance history, visit logs, staff contact notes — scattered across personal spreadsheets, email threads, and the institutional memory of whoever's been in the job longest. When that person leaves, it goes with them.

How does your PAC committee actually vote on contributions?

Reply-all email chains. SharePoint documents nobody can find. PDFs signed and scanned. If your attorney notification process involves forwarding an email and hoping, you're not alone.

What happens to your pipeline when a candidate wins?

Years of cultivation work — meetings logged, relationships built, assessments written — lives in a spreadsheet that doesn't talk to your legislative tracking system. Starting over every cycle costs you.

The Platform

Four modules.
One political program.

Every module shares a unified People CRM. A member who volunteers at a lobby day, gets cultivated as a pipeline prospect, and eventually sits on your PAC committee — that's one record, not three.

Legislative
Legislative Engagement

Track every legislator relationship — stance, visits, staff contacts, bill positions — with the reliability intelligence to know who keeps their word.

  • Legislator directory sorted by stance (Strong Ally → Strong Oppose)
  • Visit log with offline support — built for hallways, not desks
  • Bill tracking with stated, predicted, and actual vote
  • Reliability scoring: who says yes and means it
  • Lobby day briefing reports, engagement summaries
PAC
PAC Administration

The first purpose-built tool for union PAC operations. Structured contribution workflows, secure committee voting, and automated attorney notifications.

  • Candidate CRM with full contribution history
  • Contribution request workflow: submitted → reviewed → committee → approved
  • Committee members vote via secure token link — no login required
  • Attorney notification automation with audit trail
  • Expenditure account management and utilization tracking
Pipeline
Political Pipeline

Candidate cultivation is a ten-year project. GroundGame gives it a home — from first meeting to election night to their first session as a legislator you've already been tracking.

  • Kanban from On Radar → Cultivating → Candidate → Elected
  • Cohort management: Women in Politics, Next Gen Leaders, Labor Bench
  • Touchpoint logging with relationship strength tracking
  • Graduation: prospect wins election, history transfers to Legislative

The Details That Matter

Built with political operations
in mind from day one.

Multi-tenant data isolation

Every organization's data is completely isolated. Your legislator relationships, PAC records, and candidate pipeline are never visible to another org — not even your parent organization unless you grant access.

Parent org hierarchy

State councils and international staff can have read access across locals with a single login. Locals maintain full control of their own data. No shared passwords, no Excel exports.

Offline-first field tools

The visit log and event check-in are built to work without reliable cell service. Logs queue locally and sync when you're back online — because capitol hallways don't always have signal.

Role-based access control

Six distinct roles from SaaS Admin down to Read Only. PAC committee members can only see the votes they're authorized to cast. Political staff can't change org settings.

Branded public pages

Every volunteer signup page, paid event registration, and PAC contribution request form carries your logo, your colors, and your messaging — not ours.

Full audit trail

Every PAC contribution decision, committee vote, and financial action is logged with timestamp, user, and IP. When your attorney asks for documentation, it's already there.
